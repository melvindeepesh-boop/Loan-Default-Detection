Banking Terms Explained

Learn key banking terms before applying for a loan

📊

Credit Score

A number between 300–850 that represents your creditworthiness. Higher is better! Above 700 is considered good. Banks use this to decide if you qualify for a loan.

💰

Interest Rate

The percentage a bank charges you for borrowing money. For example, a 5% interest rate on a $10,000 loan means you pay $500 extra per year. Lower is better!

⚖️

DTI Ratio

Debt-to-Income Ratio. It compares your monthly debt payments to your monthly income. A DTI below 0.35 is healthy. Higher DTI means higher risk of default!

🏦

Loan Default

When a borrower fails to repay their loan on time. Defaulting damages your credit score and can lead to legal action. Our AI helps predict this risk in advance!

📅

Loan Term

The duration over which you repay the loan. Common terms are 12, 24, or 60 months. Longer terms mean smaller monthly payments but more total interest paid.

🤖

Machine Learning

AI technology that learns patterns from data. Our model was trained on 255,000+ loan records to predict default risk with 88.74% accuracy using Logistic Regression!
